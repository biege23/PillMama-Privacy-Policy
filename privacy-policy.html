PillMama Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 24, 2026

PillMama is a medication tracking and reminder app designed for users age 13 and older. The app is built to keep most user information on the device, while using a limited number of third-party services for app operations such as performance monitoring, advertising, and paid feature support.

Plain-English summary: PillMama stores medication records, reminders, history, journal entries, and related app content locally on your device. We may share limited technical and operational information with Firebase for app performance monitoring and for paid service recording and analysis. If ads or premium purchases are used, Google services such as AdMob and Google Play may also process limited information needed to deliver those features.

1. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to the PillMama app and any related support pages the developer publishes for the app.

PillMama is intended for users who are 13 years of age or older. It is not directed to children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information through a support channel, please contact the developer using the contact details shown on the app's store listing or project support page.

2. Information stored locally on your device

PillMama is designed so that the core medication data you enter remains local to your device unless you choose to export, transfer, or share it.

Medication data

Medication names, schedules, dose history, reminders, profile details, journal entries, and adherence-related records are stored locally.

Photo and proof records

Optional camera-based proof or confirmation images are stored locally as part of your private medication history unless you choose to export or transfer them.

Security settings

PIN-based security preferences and other sensitive local settings may be stored using device-secure storage when supported.

Reminder preferences

Notification schedules, alert preferences, and app appearance settings are stored locally on the device.

3. Information that may be shared with third parties

Although the app is primarily local-first, some limited information may be shared with third-party services when needed to operate, monitor, or improve the app.

Firebase and related monitoring tools

PillMama may share limited technical information with Firebase or related Google services for:

  • app performance monitoring,
  • stability, diagnostics, and troubleshooting,
  • crash or error investigation, and
  • paid service recording and analysis.

This may include technical event data, app version, device type, operating system information, performance timings, and limited purchase or entitlement status information needed to understand premium feature behavior.

Google Play Billing and paid services

If you purchase premium or other paid features, the payment itself is processed by Google Play (or the relevant store platform). The developer may receive limited purchase status information needed to confirm, restore, analyze, or support paid services.

Advertising services

The app may display ads through Google AdMob when premium features are not active. Ad providers may process device and advertising information as described in their own privacy policies in order to deliver, measure, or limit ads.

4. Permissions the app may request

Camera

Used for optional photo-based medication confirmation and related proof images.

Notifications

Used to send medication reminders, scheduled alerts, and related local notifications.

Biometric authentication

Used only when you enable local biometric unlock on a device that supports fingerprint, face, or similar authentication.

Files and sharing

Used for optional import, export, backup, PDF generation, or direct sharing of data that you choose to move.

5. Exports, transfers, and user-controlled sharing

PillMama includes optional sharing and transfer features. For example, you may choose to export or transfer local records between devices, including by local Wi-Fi transfer or share/export tools.

These actions happen only when you choose to use them. Once you export or share data outside the app, that information is subject to the privacy and security practices of the destination you choose.

6. Data retention

Local app records generally remain on your device until you edit, delete, export, overwrite, or uninstall the app.

Technical telemetry, app diagnostics, and paid service records shared with Firebase, Google Play, or advertising providers may be retained by those providers according to their own retention practices and policies.

7. Security

PillMama is designed to reduce unnecessary data sharing by keeping core user records local to the device. The app may also use secure device storage for certain sensitive settings where supported.

However, no app, device, storage system, or internet transmission method can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. You are responsible for protecting access to your device, exported files, and any shared content.

8. Your choices

  • You can choose whether to enter medication data into the app.
  • You can choose whether to use optional camera-based proof features.
  • You can choose whether to enable notifications or biometric unlock.
  • You can choose whether to export, import, or transfer local records.
  • You can avoid ad-supported use by purchasing premium where available.

9. Medical and age-appropriateness note

PillMama is intended to help users track medication activity and reminders. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

The app is designed for a general audience of users 13 and older and is not intended for children under 13.

10. Changes to this policy

This privacy policy may be updated from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will be revised when changes are made.

11. Contact

For privacy questions or support, please use the contact method listed on the app's store listing, support page, or the repository associated with the app.
